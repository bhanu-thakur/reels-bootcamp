First, the rules

How to use this bank

Five habits that turn scattered clips into a library you can actually edit from.

  1. Capture by opportunity, not by day. At a café, grab every café shot — even ones for Day 20. When fog rolls in, drop everything. The footage waits in the bank; the weather doesn't.
  2. Shoot long and steady. Record 6–12 seconds per clip and hold three still seconds at each end — editors call these handles, and you'll thank yourself.
  3. One constant per scene. Lock white balance for a location so clips match; change only ISO/exposure per shot. Shutter stays 1/50, aperture f1.6.
  4. Get coverage, not one perfect take. For any subject, bank a wide, a medium and a close. Three angles of a small thing beats one angle of a big thing.
  5. Label as you go. Right after a cluster, note it (system at the bottom). Unlabelled footage is lost footage.
