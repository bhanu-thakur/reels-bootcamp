The rhythm

How the 30 days work

Each day teaches only what that day's reel needs — so you're always shooting, never just reading.

  1. One reel a day. Today's lesson covers exactly what today's reel demands and nothing more.
  2. 10 minutes reading, 30+ minutes doing. Theory is kept to the 20% that matters; the rest is hands-on, in plain language.
  3. Difficulty rises each week, and earlier skills return in later days (spaced repetition) so they stick.
  4. Every Resolve lesson is grounded in your three guides, cited by exact lesson and page, then re-taught in plain words.
  5. Type Day 1 … Day 30 to get a lesson. Share a clip, frame or grade any time for a brutally honest critique.

Per shoot

The only dials that change

Shutter stays 1/50, aperture f1.6 — always. On each shoot you touch three things, plus frame rate.

  1. Lens — 13 / 26 / 52 mm, chosen for the feeling each shot needs.
  2. White balance (K) — a per-scene constant. Set it once for a location so every clip matches.
  3. ISO / exposure — your per-shot lever. Read the histogram and protect highlights — never let them clip.
  4. Frame rate — 24 cinematic (default), 30 standard, 60 for slow-mo. Pick per reel and know why.
